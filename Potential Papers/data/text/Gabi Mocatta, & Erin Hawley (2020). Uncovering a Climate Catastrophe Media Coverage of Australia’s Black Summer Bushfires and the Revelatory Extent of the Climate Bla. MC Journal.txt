Polling – Climate change concern 
 
January 2020 
Key results 
As part of its Climate of the Nation tracking poll, The Australia Institute surveyed 
nationally representative samples of Australians in July 2019 and January 2020 about 
climate change concerns and attitudes. The January 2020 polling also asked a new set 
of questions about climate change and bushfires.  
Key findings include: 
• Almost half of Australians (47%) are now very concerned about climate change, 
up 10 percentage points from July 2019; 
• Most Australians (57%) say that we are experiencing a lot of the impacts of 
climate change, up 14 percentage points from July 2019; 
• Four in five Australians (79%) are worried that Australia’s native forests and 
unique wildlife will never be the same; 
• Three in four Australians (73%) agree that leadership on the bushfire response 
requires the Prime Minister to lead on climate change action; 
• Seven in 10 Australians (72%) agree that this is a wake-up call for the world on 
the impacts of climate change; and 
• Two in three Australians (67%) agree that climate change is making bushfires 
worse.

Polling – January 2020 – Changing climate change concern and attitudes  
6 
In the January 2020 polling, respondents were also asked to what extent they agreed 
or disagreed with 10 statements about climate change or bushfires. 
• A majority of Australians: 
o are worried that Australia’s native forests and unique wildlife will never 
be the same (79%); 
o agree that leadership on the bushfire response requires the Prime 
Minister to lead on climate change action (73%); 
o agree that this is a wake-up call for the world on the impacts of climate 
change (72%); 
o agree that climate change is making bushfires worse (67%); 
o agree that governments should mobilise all of society to tackle climate 
change, like they mobilised everyone during the world wars (66%); 
o agree that the current bushfires demonstrate the cost of climate 
inaction (66%); and  
o agree that climate change makes bushfire hazard reduction more 
difficult to complete safely (62%). 
• More Australians agree than disagree that mining and burning coal makes 
bushfires worse (48% v 34%). 
• A majority of Australians 
o disagree that the federal Coalition government has done a good job 
managing the climate crisis (53% disagree v 33% agree); and  
o disagree that there is no connection between climate change and 
bushfires (59% disagree v 32% agree).

Method 
This study was conducted on the YouGov Galaxy Online Omnibus between Wednesday 
8 January and Sunday 12 January 2020. 
The sample comprises 1,033 Australians aged 18 years and older distributed 
throughout Australia, as follows: 
NSW 
330 
Victoria 
244 
Queensland 
219 
South Australia 
89 
Western Australia 103 
NT/ACT/TAS 
48 
 
Age, gender and region quotas were applied to the sample. 
Following the completion of interviewing, the data was weighted by age, gender and 
region to reflect the latest ABS population estimates. 
Results are shown only for larger states.
